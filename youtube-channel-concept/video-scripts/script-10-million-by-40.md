## THE 5-STEP INVESTMENT SEQUENCE (4:30 – 9:00)
**[Scene: Staircase with 5 steps, money flowing up]**

VOICEOVER:
"Here is the exact sequence. Follow it in order. Do not skip steps.

**Step 1: Emergency Fund — 1 month of expenses**
Before investing anything, have 1 month of expenses in cash.
Not 3-6 months yet. Just 1. Get this done in 30 days.

**Step 2: 401k — Up to the employer match**
If your employer matches 3%, contribute exactly 3%. This is a 100% instant return.
Annual contribution limit: $23,500 (2025). Always capture the full match first.

**Step 3: Pay off high-interest debt**
Any debt above 7% interest rate — pay it off before investing more.
A credit card at 22% APR guarantees a -22% return on money you don't use to pay it down.

**Step 4: Roth IRA — Max it out**
Contribute up to the annual limit ($7,000 in 2025, $8,000 if over 50).
Roth IRA = tax-free growth AND tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
Invest in: VTI (Total US Market ETF) or a 3-fund portfolio.

**Step 5: Back to 401k — Beyond the match**
After maxing the Roth IRA, return to the 401k and contribute up to the annual limit.
If you still have money left after maxing both: open a taxable brokerage account."

## THE INVESTMENT ALLOCATION (9:00 – 11:30)
**[Scene: Pie chart shifting from aggressive to balanced over time]**

VOICEOVER:
"What to actually buy inside these accounts.

**Ages 25–35: Aggressive Growth**
- 90% Total Stock Market (VTI or FZROX)
- 10% International (VXUS or FZILX)

Zero bonds. You have 15+ years to weather market crashes.

**Ages 35–40: Moderate Growth**
- 80% Total Stock Market
- 10% International
- 10% Bonds (BND)

Adding bonds reduces volatility as the timeline shortens.

**The Rule of Thumb: Hold your age in bonds.**
At 30: 30% bonds. At 40: 40% bonds.

This is overly conservative for most people in their 30s. I recommend staying aggressive until 40 — then shifting to this rule.

**What NOT to buy:**
- Individual stocks (unless you're willing to lose it all)
- Cryptocurrency with money you need in under 10 years
- Actively managed mutual funds (fees eat returns)
- Annuities before age 60 (expensive and inflexible)"

## THE 7 MISTAKES THAT DERAIL THE PLAN (13:30 – 15:30)
**[Scene: Red X over each mistake]**

VOICEOVER:
"Seven things that will stop you from hitting $1 million by 40:

1. **Lifestyle inflation.** Every raise gets spent instead of invested. Keep expenses flat for 12 months after every raise — invest the difference.

2. **Waiting to 'feel ready.'** The market rewards time, not timing. Every year of delay costs $50,000–$100,000 at the end.

3. **Cashing out your 401k when you change jobs.** Rolling it over is free. Cashing out costs 30–40% in taxes and penalties AND resets compound growth.

4. **Buying a house too early.** A house that costs more than 3x your annual income and ties up your capital can delay the $1M milestone by 5–10 years.

5. **Trying to time the market.** Set automatic contributions. Invest on the same day every month. Stop watching the news.

6. **Underfunding the Roth IRA in early years.** Tax-free growth for 40 years is the most powerful tool you have in your 20s.

7. **No written plan.** People without a written financial plan are 3x less likely to build wealth. Write down your monthly investment target. Track it."
