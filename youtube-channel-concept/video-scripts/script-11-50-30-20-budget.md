## WHAT IS 50/30/20 (0:30 – 2:00)
**[Scene: Paycheck splits into three buckets]**

VOICEOVER:
"The 50/30/20 rule divides your take-home pay into three buckets:

**50% — Needs**
Housing, groceries, utilities, transportation, insurance, minimum debt payments.
Things you MUST pay to function.

**30% — Wants**
Restaurants, subscriptions, entertainment, shopping, travel, hobbies.
Things you choose to enjoy.

**20% — Savings & Investments**
Emergency fund, retirement accounts, extra debt payoff, investment accounts.
Things that build your future.

That's it. Three numbers. No tracking every coffee. No 47-line spreadsheet.

It works because it's simple enough to maintain — and flexible enough to fit real life."

## WHEN 50/30/20 DOESN'T FIT (4:00 – 5:30)
**[Scene: High rent city — needs bucket overflows]**

VOICEOVER:
"Real talk: in high cost-of-living cities, 50% for needs is impossible.

If your rent alone is 45% of take-home, the standard 50/30/20 doesn't work.

**Adjustment option 1: 60/20/20**
Boost needs to 60% temporarily while your income grows. Cut wants.

**Adjustment option 2: Move the savings floor**
Never go below 10% savings, no matter what. Even at 60/30/10, you're still building.

**Adjustment option 3: Attack the income side**
If you can't fit 20% savings on your current income, the budget isn't the problem — income is.

The rule is a guide, not a law. The principle is: savings come first, wants come last."

## THE AUTOMATION TRICK (5:30 – 6:30)
**[Scene: Phone with automatic transfers set up]**

VOICEOVER:
"Here's the move that makes 50/30/20 automatic:

On payday — the same day every month — set two automatic transfers:

1. **Transfer 20% to savings account** (separate bank, slightly inconvenient to access)
2. **Transfer 30% to a 'wants' account** (this is guilt-free spending money)

The 50% needs just stay in your main account to cover bills.

When the wants account hits zero — you're done spending for the month.
When the savings account grows — your future is building itself.

You never have to decide. The system decides for you."
