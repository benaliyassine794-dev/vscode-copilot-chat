## THE ACTUAL BLOCKERS (4:30 – 7:00)
**[Scene: Lifestyle inflation animation — every raise gets spent]**

VOICEOVER:
"If income isn't the main blocker, what is?

**1. Lifestyle inflation**
The average person spends 90–100% of every raise. You get a $10,000 raise. Rent goes up. Car gets nicer. Subscriptions multiply.

Net result: same savings rate at higher income.

The fix: save 50% of every raise automatically. Spend the other 50% guilt-free.

**2. High-cost housing**
Housing is the single biggest wealth destroyer for average earners.

Spending 40–50% of take-home on rent leaves nothing to invest.

The fix: keep housing below 28–30% of gross income. Roommates, geographic arbitrage, or house hacking (renting out part of a home you own).

**3. Car trap**
The average American spends $800–$1,000/month on car expenses (payment + insurance + gas + parking).

At $50,000/year income, that's 20–24% of gross income — before taxes.

The fix: drive used, paid-off cars. Transport is a tool, not a status symbol.

**4. Debt service**
If 20–30% of take-home goes to minimum debt payments, there's nothing left to invest.

The fix: avalanche method — pay highest interest rate debt first, minimum on everything else."

## THE INCOME THRESHOLD (9:30 – 11:00)
**[Scene: Income dial with 'minimum viable' marked]**

VOICEOVER:
"Now — let's be honest. Income does matter.

There is a floor below which investing is genuinely not possible.

If you earn less than $35,000/year in a high cost-of-living area, you may need to solve income first before focusing on investing.

The sequence for low-income earners:
1. Stabilize: cover all necessities without debt
2. Build: save 1 month emergency fund ($1,000)
3. Grow income: skill up, certify, job-hop, or side hustle
4. Invest: once income reaches $40,000+, begin Roth IRA contributions

Income growth in your 20s IS a wealth-building strategy. Learning a skill, getting a certification, or adding a side income stream — these have higher ROI than any stock at this income level.

But the moment you're above the floor — investing starts. Even $50/month."
