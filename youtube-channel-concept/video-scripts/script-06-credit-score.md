## WHAT IS A CREDIT SCORE (0:40 – 2:00)
**[Scene: Credit score dial from 300-850]**

VOICEOVER:
"Your credit score is a number between 300 and 850 that tells lenders how risky you are to lend to.

The five factors that build it:
- **Payment history: 35%** — do you pay on time?
- **Credit utilization: 30%** — how much of your credit are you using?
- **Length of credit history: 15%** — how long have your accounts been open?
- **Credit mix: 10%** — do you have different types of credit?
- **New credit: 10%** — how many new accounts have you opened recently?

Miss any of these? Your score drops. Master them? 800+."

## HOW TO GO FROM 600 TO 800 (7:00 – 11:00)
**[Scene: 6-month progress bar filling up]**

VOICEOVER:
"The good news: credit scores are not permanent. They respond to your behavior within 30–90 days.

**Step 1: Pay everything on time — starting now.**
Set up autopay for every account minimum. One missed payment can drop your score 50–100 points.

**Step 2: Crush your credit utilization.**
Keep every card below 30% of its limit. Ideally below 10%.
If your card has a $5,000 limit, keep the balance under $500.

**Step 3: Don't close old accounts.**
Your oldest account is helping your score. Even if you don't use it, keep it open.

**Step 4: Request a credit limit increase.**
Call your credit card company. Ask for a higher limit. Don't spend more — but your utilization ratio improves instantly.

**Step 5: Become an authorized user.**
Ask a family member with excellent credit to add you to their oldest card. You get their history. Your score jumps.

**Step 6: Dispute errors.**
1 in 5 credit reports has an error. Check yours at AnnualCreditReport.com. Dispute anything wrong — errors that drop your score can be removed in 30 days."
