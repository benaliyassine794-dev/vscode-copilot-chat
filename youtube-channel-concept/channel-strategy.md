## The 4 Video Types Explained

### Video 1 – Tuesday: Main Educational Video (12–18 min)
The flagship content. Deep dives into one important finance topic.
Examples:
- "Why 95% of People Will Never Be Rich"
- "How Compound Interest Makes You a Millionaire"
- "The Real Cost of Owning a Home"

### Video 2 – Wednesday: Quick Money Tip (5–7 min)
Short, focused, one actionable lesson. Easy to produce, easy to watch.
Examples:
- "The 24-Hour Rule That Stops Impulse Buying"
- "One Number You Must Know Before Investing"
- "Why You Need 3 Bank Accounts"

### Video 3 – Friday: Comparison Video (10–15 min)
VS format. Viewer wants a clear winner. High click-through rate.
Examples:
- "Renting vs Buying – Who Actually Wins?"
- "Index Funds vs Individual Stocks"
- "Cash vs Credit Card – Which Should You Use?"

### Video 4 – Sunday: Myth-Busting / Story (8–12 min)
Debunk a common belief or tell a financial story. Very shareable.
Examples:
- "The Biggest Lie About Building Wealth"
- "I Lost $30,000 — Here's What I Learned"
- "5 Things Schools Never Taught You About Money"

## The 10 AM Upload Checklist

Before hitting "Publish" at 10:00 AM (applies to all 4 videos):

### Video
- [ ] Video length: 10–20 minutes (optimal for AdSense)
- [ ] No dead air longer than 3 seconds
- [ ] Hook lands in first 30 seconds
- [ ] CTA appears at 60% mark and again at the end
- [ ] End screen set up (subscribe button + next video card)
- [ ] Cards added at key moments inside the video

### Metadata
- [ ] Title: under 60 characters, curiosity-driven
- [ ] Description: 300+ words, keyword-rich, with timestamps
- [ ] Tags: 10–15 relevant tags (mix of broad + specific)
- [ ] Category: Education
- [ ] Language: English

### Thumbnail
- [ ] 1280×720 pixels, under 2MB
- [ ] Max 6 words on thumbnail
- [ ] High contrast text vs background
- [ ] Character face clearly visible
- [ ] Tested at small size (mobile preview)

### After Publishing
- [ ] Pin a comment immediately after upload
- [ ] Post a Community Tab update at 12:00 PM same day
- [ ] Reply to every comment within 48 hours

## The Perfect Video Formula

### Hook Styles That Work (First 30 Seconds)
```
TYPE 1 – Shocking Stat:
"The average American spends $700,000 on cars in their lifetime.
That's more than most people's retirement savings."

TYPE 2 – Controversial Statement:
"Your financial advisor might actually be making you poorer.
Here's the proof."

TYPE 3 – Direct Challenge:
"If you have money sitting in a savings account right now,
you're losing money. And I can prove it in 60 seconds."

TYPE 4 – Story Hook:
"Two years ago I made one financial decision that cost me $40,000.
Today I'll show you exactly what it was — so you don't repeat it."
```

### Video Structure
```
[0:00 – 0:30]   Hook — shock, challenge, or surprising stat
[0:30 – 2:00]   Problem Setup — why this matters to the viewer
[2:00 – 12:00]  Main Content — animated, step-by-step breakdown
[12:00 – 14:00] Solution + Practical Takeaways
[14:00 – 15:00] Summary + CTA (like + subscribe + next video)
```

## Analytics – What to Check Every Wednesday

| Metric | What It Tells You | Target |
|--------|------------------|--------|
| Click-Through Rate (CTR) | Is your thumbnail/title working? | 6–10% |
| Average View Duration | Is your content holding attention? | 50%+ |
| Impressions | Is YouTube pushing your video? | Growing week over week |
| Subscribers gained | Net new from this video | Always positive |
| Comments | Engagement quality | 1 per 100 views |
| Shorts views | New audience discovery | Growing |

### Monthly Review Questions
1. Which video got the most views? Why?
2. Which thumbnail had the best CTR?
3. Which video had the best watch duration?
4. What topics did viewers ask about in comments?
5. Are we on track for this month's subscriber goal?
