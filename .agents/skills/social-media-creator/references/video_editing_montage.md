# Video Editing & Montage Guide

## Core Editing Principles

**Montage** = assembling clips to create meaning, emotion, and pacing beyond any single shot.
**Golden rule:** every cut must serve the story — dead cuts kill retention.

---

## Cut Types & When to Use Them

| Cut Type     | Description                                       | Best Used For                     |
|--------------|---------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| **Hard cut** | Instant transition, no effect                     | Fast pacing, TikTok, action       |
| **J-cut**    | Next clip's audio starts before its video appears | Smooth dialogue, vlogs, interviews|
| **L-cut**    | Previous clip's audio continues into next video   | Emotional scenes, storytelling    |
| **Match cut**| Visual element in clip A matches element in clip B| Creative montage, branding moments|
| **Jump cut** | Same subject, slightly different angle/time       | Talking-head speed-up, emphasis   |
| **Cross cut**| Alternates between two simultaneous scenes        | Comparisons, suspense             |
| **Smash cut**| Sudden contrast cut (quiet → loud or vice versa)  | Humor, surprise, pattern interrupt|

## B-Roll Strategy

**B-roll** = supporting footage layered over the main talking-head or narration.

**Rule of 3:2** — for every 3 seconds of face/A-roll, aim for 2 seconds of B-roll coverage.

**What to film as B-roll:**
- Hands doing the action being described
- Environment/location shots
- Screen recordings of tools/software
- Product close-ups
- Reaction shots
- Text/graphic overlays (count as B-roll)

**Free B-roll sources:** Pexels, Pixabay, Coverr, Mixkit

## Transitions

### Natural (preferred for professional look)
- Hard cut (most used, most professional)
- L-cut / J-cut
- Whip pan: fast horizontal camera movement between clips
- Dip to black/white: strong scene separator

### Stylized (use sparingly, match platform vibe)
- Zoom transition: zoom in at cut, zoom out from next clip
- Spin/rotate: 360° rotation between clips (TikTok popular)
- Glitch: digital distortion (tech/gaming content)
- Color flash: bright white/colored frame at cut point

**Rule:** 1–2 transition types max per video. Variety = distraction.

## Color Grading Basics

### The 3 steps
1. **Correct** — fix exposure, white balance, and contrast to make footage neutral
2. **Match** — ensure all clips look consistent (same feel)
3. **Grade** — apply a creative look (warm, cool, cinematic, etc.)

### LUT (Look-Up Table)
A preset color grade. Import into DaVinci/Premiere for instant cinematic look.
- Free LUTs: Ground Control, Motion Array, RocketStock
- Popular styles: Cinematic orange+teal, Warm vlog, Clean white/airy

### Quick Color Tips by Platform
- **TikTok:** slightly saturated, high contrast — performs better in feed
- **Instagram:** airy/bright OR moody film — pick one consistent aesthetic
- **YouTube:** natural with slight warmth — feels authentic
- **LinkedIn:** clean, neutral, professional

## Captions & Subtitles

**85% of social media videos are watched WITHOUT sound.** Captions are not optional.

### Auto-Caption Tools
- CapCut: built-in auto-captions (supports 15+ languages)
- Submagic: AI captions optimized for short-form (highlight words, emojis)
- Captions.ai: iOS app, animated captions
- Descript: transcription + editing
- Adobe Premiere: Speech to Text (most accurate for multi-language)

### Caption Style by Platform
| Platform  | Style                          | Placement     |
|-----------|-------------------------------|---------------|
| TikTok    | Bold, large, center or bottom | Center screen |
| Reels     | Bold + emoji accents          | Lower center  |
| YouTube   | Standard subtitles            | Bottom bar    |
| LinkedIn  | Clean, minimal font           | Bottom        |

## Short-Form Editing Checklist (TikTok/Reels/Shorts)
- [ ] Hook established in first 0–3 seconds
- [ ] Aspect ratio: 9:16 (1080×1920px)
- [ ] No dead air or silent pauses (jump-cut them out)
- [ ] Captions added
- [ ] BGM or trending audio added and mixed (-20 to -30 dB)
- [ ] End card / CTA in last 3 seconds
- [ ] Color-corrected (consistent across all clips)
- [ ] Exported at highest quality (1080p min, 4K preferred)
- [ ] No platform watermarks (TikTok watermark banned from Instagram Reels)

## Long-Form Editing Checklist (YouTube)
- [ ] Cold open (best moment) in first 30s
- [ ] Clear chapter structure
- [ ] B-roll covers majority of talking-head footage
- [ ] Jump cuts removed from talking sections
- [ ] Color graded consistently
- [ ] End screen cards placed (last 20s)
- [ ] Exported: H.264 or H.265, 1080p min
